What to Do If You Get a Phishing Scam

Immediately disconnect from the internet to prevent further data loss and potential malware downloads. This step can stop additional personal data from being transmitted.

Change your passwords for all accounts, starting with the compromised ones. Use a strong, unique password for each account, and consider using a password manager to keep track of them.

Notify your bank and credit card companies to monitor your accounts for suspicious activity. They can help protect your accounts from fraudulent transactions.

  • Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas
  • BDO Unibank
  • BPI

Report the phishing scam to relevant authorities and your email provider. Provide them with details of the scam to help them take action and prevent others from falling victim.

  • Department of Justice (DOJ)
  • National Telecommunications Commission (NTC)
  • Department of Trade and Industry (DTI)

Use antivirus software to scan your device for malware and remove any found threats. Regular scans can help keep your devices secure.

  • Malwarebytes
  • Avast

Regularly monitor your bank and credit card statements, as well as your email and other accounts, for any unauthorized transactions or changes.

Learn more about phishing scams to recognize them in the future. Share information with friends and family to help them avoid falling victim as well.

  • Philstar Cyber Security Section
